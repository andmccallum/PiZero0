$$ \hat{E}(\omega) = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \vec{E}(t) e^{-i\omega t} dt. $$

### 1.2 Gravitational Wave Coupling

Gravitational waves (GW) are ripples in the fabric of spacetime. The strain $h(t)$ produced by a GW can be coupled with electromagnetic signals if the EM frequency modulation interacts with mass-energy distributions. In our theoretical model, the gravitational wave strain is considered as an encoding layer for thought:

$$ h(t) \propto \frac{2G}{c^4} \Delta E(t), $$

where $\Delta E(t)$ represents energy variation induced by the electromagnetic activity in the brain, $G$ is the gravitational constant, and $c$ is the speed of light.

### 1.3 Propagation Characteristics

For an electromagnetic signal encoded with fractal nuances, the propagation distance $d$ depends on power, medium, and interference, while gravitational waves propagate at the speed of light. A combined propagation model for an encoded pulse can be approached as:

$$ d_{eff} = d_{EM} + d_{GW}, $$

with both components assumed to travel at $c$ in vacuum. The strength of the signal decays per the inverse square law:

$$ I(r) \propto \frac{1}{r^2}. $$
