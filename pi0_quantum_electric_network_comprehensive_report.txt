## Mathematical Operators and Implementation

### 1. Quantum-Electric Coupling Operator (QECO)

This foundational operator establishes the coupling between quantum states and electric dynamics:

$$
\mathcal{QECO}(Q, E, t) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \alpha_i \cdot Q_i(t) \otimes E_i(t) + \Gamma_{coupling}(t)
$$

Where:
- $Q_i(t)$ represents quantum state i at time t
- $E_i(t)$ represents electric state i at time t
- $\alpha_i$ are coupling coefficients
- $\Gamma_{coupling}(t)$ is a dynamic coupling adjustment term

### 2. Adaptive Network Evolution Operator (ANEO)

This operator governs the adaptive evolution of the network structure:

$$
\mathcal{ANEO}(N, t) = N(t) \oplus \int_{0}^{t} \lambda(\tau) \cdot \nabla N(\tau) d\tau + \Omega_{adaptation}(t)
$$

Where:
- $N(t)$ represents the network state at time t
- $\lambda(\tau)$ is the adaptation rate function
- $\nabla N(\tau)$ is the gradient of network change
- $\Omega_{adaptation}(t)$ is an adaptive correction term

### 3. Quantum Entanglement Distribution Operator (QEDO)

This operator manages the distribution of quantum entanglement across the network:

$$
\mathcal{QEDO}(E, N, t) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \sum_{j=1}^{n} \beta_{ij} \cdot E_{ij}(t) \cdot \phi(d_{ij}, t) + \Psi_{distribution}(t)
$$

Where:
- $E_{ij}(t)$ represents the entanglement between nodes i and j at time t
- $\beta_{ij}$ are entanglement strength coefficients
- $\phi(d_{ij}, t)$ is a distance-dependent distribution function
- $\Psi_{distribution}(t)$ is a distribution adjustment term

### 4. Electric Field Harmonization Operator (EFHO)

This operator ensures harmonization of electric fields across the network:

$$
\mathcal{EFHO}(F, t) = \int_{\Omega} F(\vec{r}, t) \cdot \nabla \times F(\vec{r}, t) d\vec{r} + \Xi_{harmonization}(t)
$$

Where:
- $F(\vec{r}, t)$ represents the electric field at position $\vec{r}$ and time t
- $\Omega$ is the spatial domain of the network
- $\Xi_{harmonization}(t)$ is a harmonization adjustment term

### 5. Quantum-Electric Stability Operator (QESO)

This operator ensures the stability of the coupled quantum-electric system:

$$
\mathcal{QESO}(Q, E, t) = \mathcal{QECO}(Q, E, t) \otimes \mathcal{EFHO}(E, t) + \Delta_{stability}(t)
$$

Where:
- $\Delta_{stability}(t)$ is a stability adjustment term
